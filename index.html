Frequently asked questions

How does it work?
Your engineers forward to us the open-source feature requests or bugs they care about - either via email or by tagging us on GitHub/GitLab comments. Your CTO sets our priorities once every month. We then work with upstream maintainers, in-house developers and freelancers to get these tasks done with high quality and reliability. You get the improvements you want, open-source developers get paid, and the open-source ecosystem keeps improving. Everybody wins.
Why not GitHub Sponsors or TideLift or BountySource?
With Polyglot.Network, you only pay for the improvements that YOU value, not for mere support or maintenance. We take accountability and commit to a reasonable SLA. We work completely on your behalf in your interest and nobody else.
What is the pricing model?
It's quite simple. Forward us an issue URL and we will get back to you with a time and cost estimate for that specific task. The payment would be 70% for development of the feature and the remaining 30% for getting it merged into upstream.

When you're comfortable, you can start thinking of us as a polyglot open-source developer on your team. You'd buy developer-months from us as a subscription and we'll deliver equivalent work output. Because we only work on open-source, no proprietary info needs to be shared and no NDAs need to be signed. You can choose the term of the subscription to be anything between 3 months and 1 year.
What open-source projects and programming languages have you worked with?
So far, we have worked with Javascript, Pytho, Ruby, Java, Golang, Flutter, Kotlin, Swift, Clojure, Elixir, Haskell, C/C++, Rust and more. Some of the projects that we have contributed to are Django, PostgreSQL, Metabase, Ruby gems, miscellaneous React and Swift libraries. Our founders have built high-growth tech companies themselves and have more than 15+ years of experience building systems on Web/Mobile/ML.
Do you work with upstream maintainers?
Yes, that's our most preferred approach. However, as YOUR team member, we are completely dedicated to your needs and therefore, we also work with in-house developers and freelancers whenever appropriate. Our first priority is to deliver the features and bugfixes that you value.
But we want to do open-source development in house!
You're of course free to do that. You can use us for the boring open-source tasks. But fundamentally, public goods are best financed via coordination. By delegating the open-source work, we avoid the tragedy of commons and share the gains, and your team can focus on core product development - without you having to hire developers with non-core skills.
Will you end up creating and maintaining forks of upstream FOSS projects?
Our pricing model incentivizes us to merge our changes into upstream repos. However, there can be occasional conflicts between users' and maintainers' interests. In every situation, we will truly act like your team member and always do the right thing.
Is all open-source development priced at the same rate?
We will provide you effort estimate, before embarking on a complex task. The effective billing rate will depend on how many other customers are paying for work on the same open-source project. We have a sliding-scale model to appropriately price this.
What if the upstream refuses your changes?
Yes, open-source projects have certain inherent risks. For example, upstream may not want to merge a feature you have asked either because of a conflict of interest (for example, they want to protect the proprietary edition) or architectural or product disagreements. These risks exist even if your team does the open-source work in-house. But with a good volume of work, it becomes possible for us to commit to a certain proportion of guaranteed delivery. Our pricing model takes this risk into account.
